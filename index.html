Embodied AI & Robotics

I am a Master student in Electrical Engineering at the National University of Singapore (NUS), and a core member of the NUS Advanced Control Lab. My research focuses on Embodied AI and autonomous robotic decision-making.

Currently, I am working on perception and planning for aerial robots based on Vision-Language Navigation (VLN), the application of end-to-end Reinforcement Learning in complex 3D environments, and benchmarking Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for language grounding. I am also experienced in robotics development with ROS2, SLAM, and real-world deployment.

Before joining NUS, I obtained my Bachelor’s degree in Electronic and Communication Engineering from Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (NUAA).

selected publications

  1. Preprint
    AION: Aerial Indoor Object-Goal Navigation Using Dual-Policy Reinforcement Learning
    Zichen Yan, Yuchen Hou, Shenao Wang, and 3 more authors
    arXiv preprint, 2025
    Abs arXiv HTML

    We present AION, a zero-shot aerial indoor object-goal navigation system using dual-policy reinforcement learning. The system autonomously switches between exploration and target-driven navigation modes based on target visibility, leveraging RGB-D sensor input and CLIP-based multimodal features. The navigation policy is trained from scratch to master a discrete action space including take-off, navigation, and landing, and is deployed on autonomous drones using ROS 2 for real-time control.

  2. Preprint
    VLA Language Understanding Benchmark: Semantic Perturbation Evaluation
    Yuchen Hou
    Under Review, 2025
    Abs

    We discover that state-of-the-art Vision-Language-Action models achieve high benchmark scores but ignore language instructions, revealing a gap between performance and true understanding. We design a systematic semantic perturbation evaluation framework to expose language grounding failures, and propose a multi-task same-scene training approach with an augmented dataset for fine-tuning.
